Professor Wangari Maathai

The climate hero

Here is a time line of Professor Wangari's life:

  • 1940 - Born in Nyeri Kenya.
  • 1964 - Professor Wangari Maathai obtained a degree in Biological Sciences from Mount St. Scholastica College in Atchison, Kansas.
  • 1966 - She subsequently earned a Master of Science degree from University of Pittsburg.
  • 1971 - She pursued doctoral studies in Germany and the University of Nairobi, Obtaining a Ph.D.
  • 1976 - While she served in the Natinal Council of Women, she introduced the idea of planting tress with the people and continued to develop it into a broad-based, grassroots organization whose main focus is the planting of trees with women groups in order to conserve the environment and improve their quality of life.
  • 1986 - The Movement established a Pan African Green Belt Network and has exposed over 40 individuals from other African countries to approach. So far countries that have successfully launched such initiatives in Africa are Tanzania, Uganda, Malawi, Lesotho, Ethiopia, Zimbambwe, etc.
  • 1998 - She launched a campaign of the Jubilee 2000 Coalition, playing a leading global role as a co-chair seeking cancellation of the unpayable backlog debts of the poor countries in Africa by the year 2000.
  • 2002 - Professor Maathai was elected to parliament with an overwhelming 98% of the vote. She was subsequently appointed by the president, as Assistant Minister for Environment, Natural Resourses and Wildlife in Kenya's ninth parliament.
  • 2004 - Professor Wangari Maathai received numerous awards, most notably The 2004 Nobel Peace Price. OThers include The Sophie Prize, The Petra Kelly Prize for Environment, The Conservation Scientist Award and J. Sterling Morton Award among others.
  • 2011 - Professor Wangari Maathai dies at the age of 71.

"We owe it to ourselves and to the next generation to conserve the environment so that we can bequeath our children a sustainable world that benefits all."


--Professor Wangari Maathai
